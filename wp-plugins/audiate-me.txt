Highly underrated plug-in, in my opinion. Has all the features you want -- it can attach to all posts or pages, or you can manually check a box of specific posts and pages in the editor. The format is clean, and you can change the color (black works for me). You can also turn off floating button, so that users don't have to see the audio bar as they scroll down the page. The widget typically takes longer than the page to load, especially if the page is long. However, the page is accessible as soon as it loads. And the page did not seem to load slower. I did a bunch of speed tests for an hour using online tools with the widget on\/off. The results seemed to be about the same. However, I'd be curious what an expert might say about it. \n\nSimplicity here is at its finest. I really do think audio is a big asset since a lot of people don't like reading and\/or can listen to content while they do other activities. My only concern is about speed (the results of my tests notwithstanding), and it would be nice to have more voices to choose from. \n\nThis is the best free text-to-voice plugin that I have come across... It's not that I wouldn't be willing to pay some money for that right solution, but many of the bigger ones would be ridiculously expensive for a small blog. And they charge recurrent fees. \n\nAltogether, a very intriguing concept, and I just wanted to say thanks to the developer and keep up the good work for providing this free tool.
